Marketing Spend Efficiency

  • Unclassified: Average marketing spend below $500 or missing data—unable to assess efficiency.
  • Under-Leveraged: Marketing spend grew less than 80% of profit growth—likely room to increase investment.
  • Efficient: Marketing spend growth tracked profit growth within ±20% (80–120%)—good ROI.
  • Inefficient: Marketing spend grew more than 120% of profit growth—spend outpacing returns, needs review.

📊 Performance Diagnostic Distribution

Performance Diagnostic Assignment

  • Below Threshold: Small or one-off expenses whose six-month average is under $500—too minor to move the needle.
  • Cost Spike – Monitor: Expenses that jump by more than $1,000 and over 10% versus their prior-period average—worth watching for emerging cost issues.
  • Margin Risk – Investigate: Expenses are rising faster than profit (and exceed $1,000 in the most recent month)—signaling a real threat to margins that warrants immediate review.
  • Stable or Fixed – Low Concern: Regular expenses (average ≥$500) that barely change with profit (≤10% elasticity)—predictable costs with minimal margin impact.

Performance Diagnostic Definitions

  • Below Threshold: Expense is a one-time cost or has low volume (prior mean < $500)
  • Margin Risk – Continual Growth: Expense is showing consecutive months of growth and is up in the most recent month.
  • Margin Risk – Growth with Flat or Declining Profit: Expense grew while profit was flat or down—potential concern.
  • Margin Risk – Above Target: Expense grew more than 10% compared to average prior months—may require review.
  • Neutral – Keep Watching: No clear risk at this time; expense growth is not triggering other flags.
